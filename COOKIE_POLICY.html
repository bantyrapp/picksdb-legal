Last Updated: June 1, 2026

Operator. This Cookie Policy describes how PicksDb LLC (“PicksDb,” “we,” “us”) uses cookies and similar technologies on picksdb.com, our web app, and related services. Questions: support@picksdb.com. See also our Privacy Policy and Terms of Service.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your browser or device. We also use similar technologies such as local storage, session storage, and pixels (together, “cookies” in this policy). They help the site work, remember preferences, and—only if you allow—support analytics or advertising measurement.

2. Categories we use

  • Strictly necessary — Sign-in sessions, security, load balancing, consent preferences, and core site features. Always on (required).
  • Analytics — Understand how visitors use picksdb.com so we can improve performance and UX. Optional; enable in “Customize My Choices” on the site.
  • Advertising / marketing — Measure ad campaigns or show relevant ads on other platforms, if we enable those tools. Optional; we do not sell your personal data.

3. Examples on picksdb.com

  • Consent storage — remembers whether you accepted analytics or marketing cookies (picksdb_privacy_consent_v1 in local or session storage).
  • Authentication — keeps you signed in on web sessions (session cookies / tokens managed by our auth provider).
  • Preferences — theme, locale, or UI state where stored locally.
  • Payment partners — when you pay or onboard payouts, Stripe may set its own cookies under Stripe’s Privacy Policy.
  • Analytics — if enabled after you opt in, we may use privacy-oriented analytics tools; we will list major vendors here when live.

4. How long cookies last

Session cookies expire when you close your browser. Persistent cookies remain until they expire or you delete them. Your consent choice is stored until you clear site data or change preferences in our banner.

5. Your choices

  • Use the Your Privacy Choices banner on picksdb.com: Accept All, Reject All Non-Necessary, or Customize My Choices.
  • Change browser settings to block or delete cookies (may break sign-in or preferences).
  • On mobile apps, tracking is governed by your device settings and our in-app Privacy Policy—not this web banner.

6. Do Not Track

Some browsers send “Do Not Track” signals. There is no uniform industry standard; we honor applicable law and the choices you make in our consent tool.
